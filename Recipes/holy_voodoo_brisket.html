Holy Voodee Brisket

Description

Recently a gentlemen told me he followed my brisket method and turned out the best brisket he had ever made. When I asked if he seasoned with Holy Cow, he replied, “No, Holy Voodoo.” My mind was blown! I had been hearing more and more people say a Voodoo brisket was great, so we decided to put it to a proper test. And it did not fail us!

Ingredients

  • 1 full packer brisket
  • 1Meat Church Holy Voodoo Rub
  • Coarse black pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare your smoker to a temperature of 225 with oak, mesquite or hickory.
  2. Trim the full packer brisket to your liking.
  3. Season the brisket moderately with black pepper. Then season liberally with Meat Church Holy Cow. Allow to adhere for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Place the brisket in your smoker fat side up.
  5. Smoke at 225 for 7 hours.
  6. Increase the temp to 250 and smoke for 2 hours. Wrap when you obtain great bark.
  7. Then wrap in unwaxed butcher paper and return to the smoker.
  8. Increase the temp to 275 to render out those last fats. Smoke until the flat is probe tender which is typically around 203 internal temp.
  9. Remove from the smoker. Allow to rest at ambient temperature on the counter for 2 hours. At this point you can slice and eat it.
